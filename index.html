📚 Awareness & Tips

🍖 How to Feed Strays Properly

  • Use clean bowls and fresh water
  • Avoid spicy or seasoned food
  • Rice, boiled chicken, or dog food works best
  • Feed at regular times if possible

🩹 Basic First Aid

  • Clean wounds with clean water
  • Apply antiseptic if available
  • Cover with clean cloth
  • Call vet immediately for serious injuries

❤️ Why Adopt, Don't Shop

  • Save a life instead of supporting breeding
  • Strays are equally loving and loyal
  • Reduce animal homelessness
  • Adopted pets are often healthier

👩‍💻 About Paw Safe

Paw Safe was created by Tanishka Malkar with a simple mission: to protect and rescue stray animals who need our help. Every day, countless animals struggle to survive on the streets. Through this platform, we connect caring people with animals in need.

Whether you want to report a stray, adopt a furry friend, or contribute to their care, Paw Safe makes it easy to make a real difference.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I adopt a pet?

+

Browse our adoption section, select a pet you'd like to adopt, and click "Adopt Me". We'll guide you through the application process, home visit, and paperwork. The entire process typically takes 3-5 days.

What should I do if I find an injured animal?

+

Call our emergency helpline immediately at 1234-567-890. Keep the animal warm and comfortable, but don't attempt to treat serious injuries yourself. Our rescue team will arrive as soon as possible.

Can I volunteer if I have no experience?

+

Absolutely! We welcome volunteers of all experience levels. We provide training and guidance for all our volunteers. You can help with feeding, walking, fundraising, or awareness campaigns.

How are donations used?

+

100% of donations go directly to animal care: food, medical treatment, shelter maintenance, and rescue operations. We maintain complete transparency with monthly reports available on request.

Do you accept all types of animals?

+

We primarily focus on dogs and cats, but we also help other domestic animals in distress. For wildlife, we coordinate with specialized wildlife rescue organizations.
